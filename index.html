Impact of Social Media on People

Social media has definitely changed the world in the last decade. In this narrative visualization we will look at the inception of social media networks and how social media has impacted the way people spend their time.

The narrative structure being used is an interactive slideshow, which is a hybrid structure in the narrative visualization spectrum.

You can navigate through the slides either by using the "Next" and "Previous" buttons at the top or by using left and right arrow keys on your keyboard

This presentation has been tested to work on both Chrome and Firefox browser with a screen width of 1100px or higher.

Created by Sahan Serasinghe

Inception of Major Social Media Networks

We will first look at how social media networks have grown from its inception. With a total global population of 7.7 billion there are atleast 3.3 billion people online. It is reported that Facebook alone has reached 2.5 billion monthly active users.

Social media networks such as MySpace who dominated the space has disappeared by 2008 and competitors such as Facebook has remained dominant in the last decade.

The below chart depicts the growth of the prominent social media networks. You can drill down on a particular year using the slider as well as see their growth from 2004 to 2018 as an animation using the Play button.

Use of Social Media Platforms by Gender (US), 2019

For some platforms the gender differences are very large. The share of women who use Pinterest is more than twice as high as the share of men using this platform. For Reddit it is the other way around, the share of men is almost twice as high.

Daily hours spent with digital media, United States, 2008 to 2018

The increase in social media use over the last decade has, of course, come together with a large increase in the amount of time that people spend online.

As we can see, the average for the OECD is more than 4 hours per day, and in some countries the average is above 6 hours per day.

In the US, adults spend more than 6 hours per day on digital media (apps and websites accessed through mobile phones, tablets, computers and other connected devices such as game consoles). As the chart shows, this growth has been driven almost entirely by additional time spent on smartphones and tablets.
